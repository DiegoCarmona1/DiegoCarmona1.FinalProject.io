Common Farm Animal Diseases

Throughout the time you spend raising your lovely animal pals, you will fid that they can be quite dirty if not taken care of properly. That very lack of care can cost you dearly if your not careful. One of the biggest killers throughout history was disease after all. Some common farm animal based diseases include:

  • Bovine Tuberculosis: A type of TB caused by Mycobacterium Bovis bacteria. This disease spreads from contact with raw or undercooked meat, unpasteurized milk, or inhalation of the bacteria. Primarily present in bovine and other hoofed mammals, it can sp[read to human carriers. Symptoms include: weight loss, difficulty breathing, fatigue, intestinal discomfort, and some patients can have no symptoms for an extended period of time (This process known as latent TB infection).
  • Campylobacteriosis: A common bacteria spread through the environment or the feces of farm animals. Due to its commonality it has a low death rate, but is especially dangerous to those with compromised immune systems, or the young and elderly. A common symptom being diarrhea
